<p><p>Conservation Voltage Reduction (CVR) is a strategy used to lower the voltage in an electrical distribution system to reduce energy consumption and peak demand without compromising service quality. Two market types for CVR implementation are Local Control and Coordinated Control. Local Control focuses on adjusting voltage in specific areas based on real-time data, while Coordinated Control integrates multiple local systems to optimize voltage reduction across a broader region, enhancing efficiency and minimizing electrical losses while ensuring stable operations.</p></p>
