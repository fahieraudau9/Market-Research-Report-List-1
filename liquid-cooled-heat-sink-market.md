<p><ul><li>Conductable</li><li>Immersive</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The liquid-cooled heat sink market encompasses two primary types: conductable and immersive. Conductable heat sinks utilize heat-conductive fluids to transfer heat away from electronic components, enhancing cooling efficiency in systems where space is limited. Immersive cooling, on the other hand, involves submerging components directly in a thermally conductive liquid, allowing for even greater heat dissipation and uniform cooling. Both approaches aim to improve thermal management in high-performance applications, catering to the increasing demand for efficient cooling solutions in various industries.</p></p>
